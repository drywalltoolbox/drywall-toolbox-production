## Part 1: Repair Services

### What We Offer

Our repair service targets professional contractors who rely on TapeTech, Columbia, and similar automatic tapers and drywall finishing tools. The core value proposition is simple: **a $299 rebuild vs. a $1,899 new tool purchase**. That 84% savings message is our single most powerful marketing point and belongs front-and-center on every pricing surface.

### Diagnostic / Bench Fee

$50–$75. **Credited in full** toward the repair if the customer approves the quote. If they decline, they pay only the bench fee. This eliminates the "I'm afraid to send it in" hesitation.

---

### Key Pricing Principles

**Never discount publicly.** All promotional pricing, member discounts, and points credits are delivered via targeted email, logged-in account views, or checkout — never on the public pricing page. Public discounts erode perceived value and invite price shopping.

**Minimum 45% gross margin floor.** No offer, bundle, or promotion should breach this threshold. The business math only works above this floor.

**The three-tier anchor works like this:**
- The $75 Quick Fix exists to capture leads and get tools in the door
- The $299 Standard Rebuild is the product we want to sell most — priced to feel obvious
- The $499 Premium Overhaul makes $299 feel like the smart, reasonable choice

---

### Customer Trust Signals (Required on the Repair Page)

These copy elements must appear prominently and are non-negotiable for conversion:

- **"No charges until you approve your quote. Ever."**
- **"Hard Parts Price Lock"** — unquoted hard parts (chains, sprockets, shafts) are capped at 20% above the estimate. No surprise bills.
- **"Send a photo → quote in 24 hours"** — photos submitted with the repair form dramatically speed up the diagnostic process
- **90-Day Warranty** on Premium Overhaul service; 15-day on Standard
- **Sustainability note:** "Every repair saves approximately 12 lbs of metal from landfill."

---

### Repair Workflow (How It Works for the Customer)

1. Customer fills out the online intake form (brand, model, issue description, service type, photos, priority level, return shipping address)
2. **Immediate:** Automated confirmation email + prepaid inbound shipping label
3. Tool arrives at shop → "We received your [Tool]" notification
4. Tech diagnoses the issue → itemized quote emailed with a one-click approval link
5. Customer approves → repair begins
6. Repair complete → "Tool Health Report" included (parts replaced, performance metrics, recommended next service interval)
7. Outbound shipping confirmation + tracking number
8. 14 days post-delivery: CSAT survey

**Priority Levels:**
- Standard — 5–7 business days
- Expedited — 2–3 business days (+fee)
- Emergency — same/next day (when available)

## Part 2: Pro Membership (ProCare)

### Program Overview

ProCare is a three-tier annual membership program designed to reward contractors who repair tools regularly. The program drives retention, increases average order value, and generates predictable recurring revenue.

**Core philosophy:** Membership benefits are real and financially meaningful. Break-even happens quickly for active users, which makes enrollment an easy "yes" for any contractor with more than 1–2 repairs per year.

---

### Tiers at a Glance

| | Essential | Professional | Fleet |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Price** | Free | $99/year | $299/year |
| **Who it's for** | Any registered customer | Active contractors (2–5 repairs/year) | Companies with 3+ tools in service |
| **Repair Discount** | None | 10% off labor | 15% off labor + parts |
| **Return Shipping** | Customer pays | Free on repairs over $150 | Free, always |
| **Workmanship Warranty** | 15 days | 30 days | 60 days |
| **Annual Diagnostics** | Paid ($50–$75) | 1 free diagnostic/year | 2 free diagnostics/year |
| **Priority Queue** | Standard | Priority placement | Priority placement |
| **Enrollment Bonus** | — | 200 loyalty points | 200 loyalty points |

---

### Break-Even Analysis (Why Members Upgrade)

**Professional ($99/year):**
A single $299 Standard Rebuild with 10% labor discount saves approximately $30. Break-even at roughly 3–4 repairs per year — realistic for any active finisher.

**Fleet ($299/year):**
A $299 rebuild with 15% discount on labor + parts saves approximately $45 per repair. Break-even at roughly 6–7 repairs per year — realistic for any company running 3+ tools.

---

### Membership Discounts — How They Apply

- **Professional:** 10% discount applies to **labor line items only** on all repair invoices
- **Fleet:** 15% discount applies to **labor and parts** on all repair invoices
- Discount is displayed on the repair form review step before submission (for logged-in members)
- Discount is applied at invoice time, not at checkout, to allow for accurate quote generation

---

### Launch Strategy

Open with a **"Founding Member" promotion**: 50% off the first year of Professional membership for the first 90 days after launch, marketed exclusively to the existing customer email list. This seeds the program before organic growth takes hold. Without an initial push, adoption will be slow regardless of how good the program is.

**Year 1 targets:**
- 70% of enrolled members at Essential (free — lead capture)
- 25% at Professional
- 5% at Fleet
- 70% renewal rate at 12 months

**Revenue note:** Membership revenue is real, but it requires active marketing. Technology alone does not drive enrollment. Plan for a parallel contractor outreach campaign.

## Part 3: Loyalty Points (Rewards Program)

### Program Overview

All registered customers are automatically enrolled. Points are earned on every qualifying order and redeemed as account credit at checkout. The program is designed to increase repeat purchase frequency without creating a margin drain.

**One rule that drives everything:** Always translate points into dollar value at every display. Customers disengage from abstract point counts. They act on dollar savings. Every time points appear on screen, show the dollar equivalent.

---

### Earning & Redeeming

| | Rate |
|---|---|
| **Earn rate** | 1 point per **$2.00 spent** (pre-tax, pre-shipping) |
| **Redemption value** | 100 points = **$5.00** account credit |
| **Effective return** | 2.5% of eligible spend |
| **Minimum to redeem** | 100 points ($5.00) |
| **Maximum per transaction** | 5,000 points ($250.00) |
| **Redemption increment** | 100 points ($5.00) per step |
| **Expiration** | 24 months from earning date |
| **Expiry extension** | Any earning or redemption activity extends all points by 6 months |

**Why this rate:** At 1 point per $1 with 100 pts = $5, the effective return would be 5% — unsustainable against repair orders with parts-heavy margins. At 1 point per $2, the effective return is 2.5%, and the net program cost is approximately 1.5% of revenue at a 60% redemption rate. That's financially comfortable and still meaningful to the customer.

---

### What Earns Points

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Repair services (labor + parts) | 1 pt per $2 spent |
| Product / parts orders | 1 pt per $2 spent |
| DIY repair kits | 1 pt per $2 spent |
| Membership fees | 0 pts (separate value prop) |
| Shipping charges | 0 pts |
| Refunded / cancelled orders | Points reversed |

---

### Bonus Points Events

Bonus events are used **tactically** — not constantly. Overuse devalues the base program.

| Event | Bonus |
|---|---|
| First repair ever | 2× points on that order |
| Pro or Fleet membership enrollment | 200 flat bonus points |
| Referring a new customer | 400 pts to referrer / 100 pts to new customer |
| Seasonal promotion (max 2×/year) | 1.5× points on all repairs |
| Multi-tool bundle (2+ tools in one shipment) | +50 flat points per additional tool |

Bonus points are promoted via post-login dashboard banners and email only — never on the public-facing website.

---

### How Redemption Works at Checkout

1. Logged-in customer arrives at checkout with a points balance
2. Site displays: *"You have 850 points = **$42.50** in credit — Apply to this order?"*
3. Customer uses a stepper to choose how many points to apply (in 100-pt increments)
4. Chosen amount is applied as an account credit against the order total
5. Order confirmation shows points applied + new balance + points earned on this purchase

---

### Points Expiration & Liability Management

Points outstanding are a real financial liability. Management approach:

- Points expire 24 months after the date they were earned
- Any account activity (earning or redeeming) resets the expiration clock by 6 months
- Warning emails go out at 18 months and again at 23 months: *"Your points expire in 6 months"*
- At program maturity (~200 customers, 60% active), estimated outstanding liability is approximately **$3,600** — entirely manageable

---

### How Points Are Communicated (Required Language)

| Context | Use This | Not This |
|---|---|---|
| Balance display | "1,250 points = **$62.50** in credit" | "You have 1,250 points" |
| Earning confirmation | "You earned **150 points ($7.50 value)**" | "150 points added" |

| Program page | "Earn 1 point for every $2 spent. **100 points = $5 off.**" | "Earn points on purchases" |
| Balance email | "Your balance: **850 points = $42.50** — expires April 2028" | "You have 850 reward points" |

## Key Principles Across All Three Programs

**Points ≠ Membership.** These are intentionally separate programs with separate values. Members earn points at the same base rate as non-members. The simplicity is a feature — don't cross the streams.

**Transparency wins.** Itemized invoices, before/after repair notes, Tool Health Reports, video summaries — every one of these differentiates us from competitors who offer "send it in, here's a number."

**Never discount publicly.** All discounts — membership, points, bundles, promos — are delivered privately. This rule applies to all three programs.

**The minimum margin floor is 45%.** No pricing, offer, or program should break this. The financial model for all three programs is built on it.
